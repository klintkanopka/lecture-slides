# Recap

<v-clicks depth="2">

- Positive continuous outcomes often call for Gamma or inverse Gaussian GLMs
- Gamma and inverse Gaussian models are useful when outcomes are strictly positive and right-skewed
- Multilevel models help when observations are nested within groups or repeatedly measured
- In `R`, `lmer()` fits multilevel linear models and `glmer()` extends them to GLMs
- Next week: building, fitting, diagnosing, and evaluating multilevel models
